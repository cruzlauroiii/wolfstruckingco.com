CDL-A vs CDL-B: Which License Pays More in 2026?

Published April 5, 2026 · 6 min read · By Wolfs Trucking Co.

Short answer: CDL-A pays more — typically $10,000-$15,000/year more than CDL-B. But the real answer depends on home time, route type, endorsements, and your life situation.

Here's a complete breakdown based on 2026 pay data from Southern California fleets, including our own.

CDL-A: Higher Pay, Less Home Time

A Class A CDL lets you drive combination vehicles (tractor + trailer). This opens up regional and over-the-road (OTR) work — the highest-paying trucking segments.

CDL-A Pay in 2026 (Southern California)

  • Regional (home weekly): $65,000 - $80,000
  • OTR (home every 2-3 weeks): $75,000 - $95,000
  • Dedicated lanes: $70,000 - $85,000
  • Port drayage: $55,000 - $75,000 (plus union benefits often)
  • Tanker/Hazmat: $85,000 - $110,000 with endorsements

CDL-A Endorsement Value (2026)

Endorsements add meaningful pay:

  • H (Hazmat): +$3,000-$5,000/year
  • N (Tanker): +$5,000-$8,000/year
  • T (Doubles/Triples): +$2,000-$4,000/year
  • X (Tanker + Hazmat): +$8,000-$12,000/year
  • TWIC (port access): +$3,000-$5,000/year for drayage

CDL-B: Steady Pay, Home Daily

A Class B CDL is for single-unit vehicles (straight trucks, delivery trucks, some buses). The pay is lower but the lifestyle is often better for drivers with families.

CDL-B Pay in 2026 (Southern California)

  • Local delivery: $55,000 - $65,000
  • LTL multi-stop: $58,000 - $68,000
  • Beverage/vending: $60,000 - $72,000
  • Refuse/sanitation: $65,000 - $85,000 (often union, with pension)
  • Dump truck: $52,000 - $65,000
Hidden value of CDL-B: Home every night. No hotel living. No DOT 14-hour on-duty pressure. For many drivers with kids, this is worth the $10K/year pay gap.

Total Compensation: What Actually Matters

Base salary tells part of the story. Here's what to compare when evaluating CDL jobs:

Benefits That Move the Needle

  • 401(k) match: A 4% match on $70K is $2,800/year
  • Health insurance: $10,000-$15,000/year value for family coverage
  • PTO: 2 weeks = $2,700 at $70K. 3 weeks = $4,000
  • Sign-on bonus: $3,000 is common. Some carriers offer $5,000-$10,000
  • Safety bonus: $500/quarter at Wolfs Trucking. Adds up to $2,000/year
  • Fuel efficiency bonus: $100-$500/month for MPG performance

Red Flags in CDL Job Listings

  • "Up to $95,000" — means very few drivers actually hit that. Ask for median.
  • "1099 / independent contractor" — you pay your own taxes, insurance, truck expenses. True W-2 is almost always better.
  • "Lease-to-own program" — almost never works out in the driver's favor. Classic predatory setup.
  • "Weekly miles guaranteed" — fine, but ask what the minimum is if weather or load availability drops.

Career Progression Path

Here's how CDL drivers typically move up in pay:

  1. Year 1: New CDL-A or CDL-B. $55K-$65K depending on class.
  2. Year 2-3: Add endorsements (Hazmat, Tanker). Move to regional or dedicated. $65K-$75K.
  3. Year 4-5: Senior driver status. Training bonus, safety bonus maxed out. $75K-$90K.
  4. Year 6+: Driver trainer, fleet manager, or dispatcher. Salaried roles $70K-$100K.

Which License Should You Get?

Get CDL-A if:

  • You want to maximize pay
  • You're OK being away from home weekly or longer
  • You want route variety (regional, OTR, dedicated)
  • You plan to add endorsements for higher pay
  • You might want OTR flexibility for travel

Get CDL-B if:

  • You want to be home every night
  • Family/local routine is a priority
  • You like structured Monday-Friday schedules
  • You're interested in beverage, delivery, or municipal work
  • Training cost is a constraint
Pro tip: Get CDL-B first if you're unsure. It's cheaper, faster, and you can upgrade to CDL-A later (most programs offer 4-6 week upgrade courses). Many drivers start CDL-B local, build experience, then go CDL-A for higher pay.

Summary

CDL-A pays $10K-$15K more per year than CDL-B, but requires more training and typically less home time. CDL-B trades pay for daily home time. Both are solid careers with upward mobility.

The best-paying CDL-A segments in 2026 are tanker/hazmat ($85K+) and OTR ($75K+). The best-paying CDL-B segments are refuse/sanitation ($65K+) and beverage ($60K+).
