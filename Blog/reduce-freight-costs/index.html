How to Reduce Freight Shipping Costs 20% (Skip the Broker)

Published March 28, 2026 · 7 min read · By Wolfs Trucking Co.

If you're shipping freight through a broker, you're almost certainly overpaying. Not because brokers are bad people — they provide a useful service for occasional, unpredictable loads. But for any shipper with consistent volume, the 15-30% broker markup is pure cost you can eliminate.

Here's the real data, and the 5-step path to lower rates.

How Freight Brokerage Actually Works

A broker doesn't own trucks. They match shippers with carriers, taking a percentage for the introduction. Here's what happens when you call a broker for a 100-mile load:

  1. You call the broker and ask for a quote.
  2. The broker calls 3-5 carriers to get their rates.
  3. The cheapest carrier says $145 ($1.45/mi).
  4. The broker adds 15-30% markup and quotes you $180-$190.
  5. They book the cheapest carrier at $145 and pocket the $35-$45 difference.

Multiply that by 500 loads per year at $40 markup each: $20,000 in annual savings you're leaving on the table.

The 5-Step Path to Direct-Carrier Pricing

Step 1: Audit Your Current Rates

Pull 6 months of freight invoices. Calculate:

  • Cost per mile by lane
  • Which loads had the highest markup (look for anything over $2.00/mile for standard routes)
  • Which carriers are actually hauling your loads (look at the BOL, not the invoice)

If the carrier on the BOL is different from who invoiced you, you're going through a broker.

Step 2: Identify Your Consistent Lanes

Direct-carrier relationships work when you have predictable volume. Look for:

  • Same origin → same destination, 3+ times/month
  • Same day of week or time of day
  • Same cargo type (dry van, reefer, flatbed)

Those are your "dedicated lane candidates." Direct carriers will compete hard for these.

Step 3: Contact 3 Local Carriers Directly

For each lane, find 3 carriers who actually own trucks in your area. Check:

  • FMCSA SAFER (safer.fmcsa.dot.gov) for their DOT/MC numbers, safety scores, fleet size
  • Google reviews
  • Ask for real customer references in your industry

A carrier with 20-100 trucks and a clean CSA score is often the sweet spot — small enough to care, big enough to be reliable.

Step 4: Negotiate on Your Best Lane First

Don't try to move everything at once. Start with ONE lane where you have predictable volume. Ask:

  • Rate: Target 15% below your current broker rate
  • Volume commitment: What do they want monthly to guarantee the rate?
  • Accessorials: Lock in detention ($50/hr), stop fees ($25), tarp fees
  • Service level: On-time target (95%+ standard), damage claim rate

Step 5: Scale Gradually

Once Lane 1 is working (typically 60 days), move Lane 2, then 3, etc. Keep 10-20% of volume with brokers for unpredictable or backup capacity — you'll still need them occasionally.

Real example: One of our Acme Corp clients moved monthly drayage from broker to direct after an audit. Previous broker rate: $335. Our direct rate: $285. Annual volume: 240 containers. Annual savings: $12,000.

When You Should Use a Broker

Brokers are still useful for:

  • One-off loads where the relationship cost outweighs the savings
  • Unusual cargo where your regular carrier doesn't specialize
  • Emergency capacity when your regular carrier is full
  • Lanes you don't have volume in (brokers shop the whole market)

Most shippers end up with 80% direct / 20% broker. That's the right mix.

Other Cost-Reduction Levers

Consolidate LTL Stops

If you have 4 separate LTL shipments going similar directions, consolidate into one multi-stop route. Saves $100-$300 per consolidation.

Schedule Deliveries Efficiently

Morning pickups and afternoon deliveries get better rates than rush/off-hour slots. Plan 48 hours ahead when possible.

Weigh Your Loads Accurately

Declaring under-weight triggers surcharges that double the savings you'd get from accurate declaration. Get a certified scale reading.

Negotiate Detention Rates Up Front

Standard is $50/hr after 2 hours free. Some brokers quote $75-$100/hr detention. Lock in $50 before booking.

Consider Volume Discounts

Most carriers offer 10%+ discount at 10 loads/month, 15%+ at 50 loads/month. Even if you don't hit the tier, asking often gets you 5%.
