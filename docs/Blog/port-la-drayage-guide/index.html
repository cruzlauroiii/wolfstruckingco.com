Port of LA drayage guide

Yard congestion, chassis pools, TWIC checks — the full playbook.

The Port of LA + Long Beach moves ~25M TEUs a year — about a third of US import volume. Drayage looks simple from the outside (truck container in, truck container out) but it's gated by 5 things that catch new operators flat-footed.

1. TWIC card

Transportation Worker Identification Credential. Federally required for terminal access. ~$125 fee, 6-week processing. Without one you cannot get past the gate.

2. Chassis pool membership

POLA/POLB don't supply chassis. You either own/lease or are a member of a pool (FlexiVan, DCLI, TRAC). Pool membership is the cheapest entry point at ~$0/day rental but $25-$50/day if you keep it past free time.

3. eModal & PortDrayage appointment systems

Every terminal uses an appointment platform. Same-day appointments are gold. Show up without one and you may sit 4-6 hours. We use eModal for APM Terminals and Pier 400 and have appointment alerts auto-text our drivers.

4. Yard congestion

Tuesday/Thursday afternoon are worst. Best bet: 4am-8am or 8pm-midnight. Wolfs schedules half our drayage off-peak.

5. Pre-pull strategy

Pull from terminal to your yard, decouple, deliver to consignee on a separate run. Better truck utilization, lower demurrage risk.
