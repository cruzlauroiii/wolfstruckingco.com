How to cut freight costs 18%

Three vendor consolidation moves a beverage shipper made to save $280K/yr.

BevCo (anonymized customer) was paying 5 different drayage carriers + 2 LTL carriers + 1 reefer specialist. Their finance team thought they had "spread risk." Their ops team knew it was a mess.

Move 1: Single-source LA-LB drayage

Consolidating drayage onto one dedicated carrier with a published rate card cut the per-container cost 14%. It also eliminated the appointment-stacking problem (5 carriers each booking the same 7 AM slot).

Move 2: Reefer + dry into one carrier

BevCo had reefer SKUs and dry SKUs. Different carriers meant different rate cards, double-booked stops, and 2 invoices to reconcile. One carrier with a mixed fleet = single rate card, fewer no-shows.

Move 3: Cross-dock instead of direct

Pull from terminal to our yard, decouple, then deliver. Saves chassis demurrage and lets us batch deliveries. Net: same arrival time at customer, $1.20/case lower freight cost.

Result: $280K annual savings, 99.1% on-time vs 91% before, single quarterly invoice.
