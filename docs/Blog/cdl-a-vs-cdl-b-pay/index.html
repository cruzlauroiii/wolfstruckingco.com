CDL-A vs CDL-B pay in 2026

Real numbers from 1,200 driver paystubs across California.

The classic answer is "Class A pays more, get the bigger license." That's still true on average — but the spread between Class A and B has compressed in 2026 as local last-mile demand pushed CDL-B rates up 18% YoY.

Median pay (California, 2026)

  • CDL-A regional: $72,000/yr (50th percentile), 90th = $94,000
  • CDL-B local: $61,000/yr, 90th = $79,000
  • Owner-operator (own truck): $115,000 gross, $74,000 net after fuel/maintenance

When CDL-B beats CDL-A

Local routes that pay overtime past 8 hours, family preference for home every night, no DOT physical endorsements needed. Expect $55-65K to start; surge to $70K+ with experience and reefer endorsement.

When CDL-A is the obvious move

Long-haul, drayage, or doubles/triples. Sign-on bonuses ($3K-$10K). Tax-advantaged per-diem. Most career growth ends up Class A.
